# Product Bible

## Product Vision

NextBite is a real-world food exploration game. It helps people discover restaurants by going outside, collecting experiences, and reducing the stress of deciding what to eat.

NextBite is not a food delivery app, calorie tracker, OpenRice clone, or Google Maps clone.

## Mission

Make deciding what to eat feel curious, playful, and clear.

The target feeling is:

> I wonder what I will discover today.

## Target Users

- Age 18-35.
- People who like cafes, restaurants, food videos, and lifestyle discovery.
- Users who save restaurants from Instagram, TikTok, and Xiaohongshu.
- People who often do not know what to eat.
- People who care about health without wanting a clinical nutrition product.
- Users who enjoy collecting experiences and visible progress.

## Core Philosophy

- Exploration before search.
- Decision-making before browsing.
- Food before interface.
- Health as a quiet signal, not a lecture.
- Game before productivity.
- Progress as pride, not pressure.

## Product Principles

- Reduce decision fatigue.
- Keep every screen focused on one primary purpose.
- Make the map feel like the world, not a utility panel.
- Use food photography and place context as the emotional driver.
- Prefer simple, durable interactions over decorative novelty.
- Preserve a premium, friendly, timeless tone.

## Core Features

### Explore

Current implementation focuses on Explore as the foundation:

- Full-screen Mapbox map.
- User location permission.
- User location puck.
- Camera follow mode.
- Local mock restaurant markers.
- Marker tap opens a restaurant bottom sheet.

### Restaurant Detail

Planned product responsibility:

- Help users decide what to order at a specific place.
- Prioritize food photography, appetite, and concise health context.

### Saved

Planned product responsibility:

- Personal collection of restaurants users want to visit.
- Future import path from Instagram, TikTok, and Xiaohongshu.

### Draw

Planned product responsibility:

- Signature food decision feature.
- Should feel like opening a collectible card, not using a random generator.

### Social

Planned product responsibility:

- Encourage healthy habits with friends.
- More like Strava than social media.

### Profile

Planned product responsibility:

- Celebrate progress, achievements, walking history, and food collection.

## UX Philosophy

Each screen should create one primary emotion:

- Explore: curiosity.
- Restaurant: appetite.
- Draw: excitement.
- Saved: anticipation.
- Social: motivation.
- Profile: pride.

If a screen communicates information but fails to create its intended emotion, the screen is not finished.

## Success Metrics

Initial product metrics should focus on behavior and retention:

- Location permission opt-in rate.
- Explore session starts.
- Restaurant marker taps.
- Bottom sheet open rate.
- Walk There taps.
- Saved restaurant count.
- Draw interactions.
- Repeat weekly active users.
- Qualitative signal: users describe the app as discovery, not search.
